Interlocking Method

Interlocking is a general method to connect parts into a structurally stable assembly purely based on their geometric arrangement. In an interlocking assembly, there is only one movable part, called the key, while all other parts as well as any subset of parts are immobilized relative to one another. Starting from the key, the assembly can be gradually disassembled into individual parts by following a specific order.

Interlocking assemblies have intriguing properties, including fastener-free, structurally stable, disassemblable, and ease-of-assembly. They have been designed in various geometric forms and practiced in a wide variety of applications, including puzzles, furniture, architecture, and personalized fabrication.

This topic studies the theory, analysis methods, and design algorithms of interlocking assemblies. The goal is to explore the potential of interlocking assemblies for being used in modern digital manufacturing and advanced construction.
